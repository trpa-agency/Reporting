What’s a residential allocation?

A residential allocation is a permission slip from TRPA that lets a property owner build one new residential unit in the Lake Tahoe Basin. The Regional Plan caps how many can ever be built — 8,687 cumulative since 1987 — to keep development paced with the basin’s environmental capacity.

Each county and the City of South Lake Tahoe gets a pool of allocations to distribute as new home permits get approved. When a pool runs out, the jurisdiction has to wait for the next round, transfer development rights from elsewhere, or use bonus units (a separate program for affordable housing). This page shows what each pool has left as of the most recent TRPA report.

Looking for a specific parcel’s allocation history? parcels.laketahoeinfo.org has the per-APN detail.
