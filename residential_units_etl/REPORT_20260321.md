## 1. Purpose

This ETL populates a parcel history feature class with annual residential unit counts sourced from a master CSV file, covering the years 2012–2025. The feature class supports time-series analysis by Town Center, TAZ, Local Plan area, Zoning district, Regional Land Use category, and bonus unit boundary.

### Step 2 — Load CSV and Build Lookup

The CSV is melted from wide to long format and a `csv_lookup` dictionary is built: `(APN, Year) → residential_units`.

**El Dorado County APN Format Fix:**
El Dorado County (COUNTY = 'EL') changed its APN suffix format in 2018, adding a leading zero (e.g., `080-155-11` → `080-155-011`). The CSV predates uses the 2-digit format for all years. The ETL detects which APNs need correction by comparing the format of El Dorado APNs already present in the FC (which uses the authoritative assessor format per year) and applies bidirectional padding:

- **APNs to pad** (Year ≥ 2018, CSV has 2-digit suffix, FC has 3-digit): **19,381 APNs**
- **APNs to de-pad** (Year < 2018, CSV has 3-digit suffix, FC has 2-digit): **506 APNs**
- **Total CSV rows corrected:** 158,084

**Result:** `csv_lookup` with **590,268 entries**.

---

### Step 3 — APN Crosswalk

Some APNs in the CSV do not appear in the FC for certain years due to parcel splits, merges, and renumbering events. For these cases, the ETL attempts to find the correct FC APN by spatially joining the historical APN's centroid to the year-specific parcel layer.

**Process:**
1. Identify all APN × Year combinations present in the CSV but absent from the FC.
2. For APNs with available geometry (from any year they appear in the FC), compute polygon centroids and create an in-memory point FC.
3. Per year, run two spatial join passes:
   - **Pass 1 (WITHIN):** Centroid falls within a parcel polygon in that year.
   - **Pass 2 (CLOSEST ≤ 50 m):** For any centroid not matched by Pass 1, find the nearest parcel within 50 meters.
4. Extend `csv_lookup` with `(FC_APN, Year) → units` entries for all crosswalk matches.

These are likely APNs newly added to the CSV for recent years that do not yet appear in the FC parcel history, or newly created parcels for 2023+ that are not yet in the source `Parcel_History_Attributed` dataset.

578 unique APNs are present in the CSV with units > 0 but show 0 units in the FC for one or more years.

**PARCEL_SPLIT (453 APNs / 4,203 units):** The APN is present in the FC for some years but disappears — typically because it was subdivided or renumbered. The CSV continues to reference the old APN. This is the dominant issue. The crosswalk resolves cases where the old centroid falls within a new successor parcel, but it cannot resolve condo-type splits where one parent parcel becomes 10–150 individual unit parcels, as the centroid can only match to one successor.

**UNKNOWN (111 APNs / 667 units):** The APN appears in the FC for some years but has 0 units for the flagged years with no clear split pattern. Requires manual investigation. May include data entry errors in the CSV (wrong APN), parcels with complex histories, or edge cases in the crosswalk.

**PARCEL_NEW (14 APNs / 232 units):** These APNs never appear in the FC for any year across the full 2012–2025 range. They are referenced in the CSV but have no corresponding geometry in the parcel history dataset.

**TOWN_CENTER (95.5% null):** Expected. Town centers are small, dense geographic zones covering a small fraction of the TRPA basin. The vast majority of parcels — particularly rural, lakefront, and mountain residential areas — are not within a designated town center. This is a correct result.

**Other fields (~71% null):** The ~71% null rate is consistent across TAZ, LocalPlan, Zoning, and RegionalLandUse. This warrants investigation. Two hypotheses:

1. **Service coverage gap:** The spatial services may not cover the full TRPA basin — e.g., the Zoning or LocalPlan layer may only cover incorporated jurisdictions or areas with adopted plans.
2. **Parcel years without attributes:** Since the FC has 14 copies of each parcel (one per year) and the spatial join assigns values based on 2025 service extents, parcels in areas where coverage has expanded over time may show nulls for earlier years even though they would be covered today.

This should be validated by spot-checking a sample of null parcels against their known jurisdiction in ArcGIS Pro.

## 5. Recommended Next Steps

### Priority 1 — CSV Corrections for Lost APNs

Open `QA_Lost_APNs` in ArcGIS Pro and sort by `Total_Units_CSV` descending. Focus on:

- **PARCEL_SPLIT APNs with the highest unit counts** — these are the biggest contributors to the deficit. For each, determine the successor APN(s) and update the CSV to reference them for the years after the split. The `Suggested_Action` field in the table provides the year of last FC appearance as a starting point.
- **PARCEL_NEW APNs** — 14 APNs with 232 total units that have never appeared in the parcel fabric. Verify whether these parcels exist under a different APN format or whether they represent data entry errors in the CSV.
- **UNKNOWN APNs** — 111 APNs that appear in the FC for some years but show 0 units for flagged years with no clear pattern. These require individual review.

### Priority 2 — Investigate 2023–2025 Deficit Spike

The deficit jumps from ~-52 in 2022 to ~-200+ in 2023–2025, an increase of approximately 150 additional missing units that is not explained by the parcel split pattern from earlier years. Steps to investigate:

1. Filter `QA_Lost_APNs` to APNs where `Years_In_FC = 'NEVER'` AND the years lost are exclusively 2023, 2024, or 2025.
2. Check whether those APNs exist in the AllParcels service for 2023+ — if so, the source `Parcel_History_Attributed` may not have been refreshed with the most recent year's parcels.
3. If the parcels are new subdivisions or newly recorded lots, they may need to be added to the source GDB before the next ETL run.

### Priority 3 — Spatial Completeness Investigation

The ~71% null rate for TAZ, Zoning, LocalPlan, and RegionalLandUse among TRPA-boundary rows needs clarification:

1. In ArcGIS Pro, select a sample of null rows (e.g., `WITHIN_TRPA_BNDY = 1 AND ZONING_ID IS NULL`) and display them on the map alongside the Zoning service layer to determine whether the null represents a genuine coverage gap or a service extent issue.
2. If the Zoning/LocalPlan services only cover specific jurisdictions, the null rate may be correct for parcels in unincorporated or non-participating jurisdictions.
3. If the coverage gaps are significant, consider supplementing with alternative data sources (e.g., county GIS layers) for uncovered areas.

### Priority 4 — Annual Update Workflow

When the CSV is updated for a new year (e.g., adding 2026 data):

1. Add the new year column to `config.py` → `CSV_YEARS` list.
2. Verify the new year's layer index in the `YEAR_LAYER` mapping in `config.py`.
3. Run the full ETL without `--skip-s01` to rebuild the FC with the new year included.
4. Review `QA_Lost_APNs` for any new APNs that need crosswalk or CSV correction.

If only unit values are being updated (no new year), run with `--skip-s01` to save ~1 minute.

### Priority 5 — TownCenter Field Validation

TOWN_CENTER was populated from the `Name` field of the TownCenter boundaries service (`Boundaries/FeatureServer/1`). Spot-check a sample of parcels within known town center boundaries (e.g., South Lake Tahoe, Incline Village) in ArcGIS Pro to confirm the values are correct and consistent with TRPA planning geography definitions.

## 7. Known Limitations

- **Condo/unit splits:** When the CSV references a parent parcel APN that has since been subdivided into individual condo units (each with its own APN in the FC), the crosswalk can only match the centroid to one successor parcel. The remaining units are lost. This is a structural limitation — the CSV must be updated to reference individual unit APNs for these cases.
- **14-copy join overhead:** Because the FC has 14 rows per parcel (one per year), spatial attribute joins assign the same attribute to all 14 rows based on 2025 service coverage. Historical coverage differences (e.g., a zoning change mid-period) are not captured.
- **Service availability:** All spatial joins depend on live TRPA ArcGIS services. If a service is unavailable at run time, the corresponding rows will receive null values silently. Adding service availability checks before Step 5 would improve reliability.
- **Minor run-to-run variation:** Unit totals vary by ±1–3 per year between runs due to spatial join tie-breaking at parcel boundaries. This is inherent to centroid-based joins and does not represent a data integrity issue.
